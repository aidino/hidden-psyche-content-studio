## Brand Voice

- **Tone**: Reassuring, intellectual, subtly exclusive — empowering, never condescending
- **Style**: Validating the viewer's experience by reframing their quirks as strengths
- **Key Phrases**: "You're not difficult, you're wired differently", "This isn't arrogance, it's...", "You're not broken, lazy, or weird"
- **Anti-Superficial Stance**: Praises "deep thinkers" while criticizing "small talk," "fake motivation," and "surface level living"
- **Avoid**: Overly academic jargon without explanation, generic motivation, moralizing
- **Content feel**: "I finally feel understood" moments

## Hook Style: Relate, Reframe, Reveal

The first 30 seconds of every script follow this strict formula:

1. **The Relatable Question** — Address a common but isolating feeling.
   - *"Have you ever noticed that some people struggle to make friends no matter where they go?"*
   - *"Have you ever felt tired after talking to someone but you couldn't explain why?"*

2. **The Misconception Reframe** — Immediately state that society's negative assumption is wrong.
   - *"People think being highly intelligent makes life easier but psychology shows..."*
   - *"It's not always about looks, it's not always about confidence..."*

3. **The Psychological Promise** — Promise to reveal the hidden truth using science.
   - *"Let's break down what really goes on inside the mind..."*
   - *"Here are the hidden psychological struggles..."*

---

## Script Structure: Numbered List with Jargon

The body is a structured listicle (usually 5–12 points). Each point must contain:

1. **The Trait** — State the behavior clearly.
   - *"One: they avoid vulnerability"*
   - *"Two: they overanalyze social interactions"*

2. **The Scientific Jargon** — Introduce a real psychological term for authority.
   - Frequently used: *cognitive complexity, metacognition, Dunning-Kruger effect, hedonic adaptation, internal locus of control*

3. **The Empowering Explanation** — Reframe the trait as a positive.
   - Tell the viewer their quirk means their brain processes reality more deeply, or they're protecting their peace.

## Title Formulas

### Formula 1: "The Psychology of..."
Focus on a specific, slightly unusual behavior.
- The Psychology of People Who Don't Post Their Photos on Social Media
- The Psychology of People Who Treat Their Birthday Like a Normal Day
- The Psychology of People Who Hate Small Talk
- The Psychology of Quiet People

### Formula 2: "Numbered Signs/Habits"
Appeal to the viewer's desire to diagnose themselves with positive or rare traits.
- 7 Signs You Have an Extremely Rare Personality
- 8 Signs You're Too Smart for Most People to Handle
- 8 Signs You Absorb the Emotions of a Room

### Formula 3: Authority Parenthetical
Always legitimize the claim at the end of the title.
- (Psychology Explains)
- (Psychology Says So)
- (Backed by Psychology)
