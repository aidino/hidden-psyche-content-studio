# Design System

## Overview

VnExpress news reports employ a clean, authoritative, and structured design aesthetic. The visual personality leans on high-contrast textual components, sparse but impactful brand colors (deep red), and a mix of serif and sans-serif typography to distinguish between headlines and body copy. Motion is pragmatic, avoiding unnecessary fluff, focusing on delivering information directly and credibly.

## Colors

- **Brand Primary (VnExpress Red)**: `#9F224E` — used for primary headers, CTAs, and active indicators.
- **Secondary Red**: `#B42652` — used for hover states and secondary highlights.
- **Surface Background**: `#FFFFFF` — clean white background for high readability.
- **Subdued Background**: `#FCFAF6` — off-white for secondary sections or quote areas.
- **Primary Text**: `#222222` — deep black/grey for primary headings and body copy.
- **Secondary Text**: `#757575` — muted grey for dates, metadata, and breadcrumbs.

## Typography

- **Serif Font**: Merriweather. Used for major headlines and authoritative statements (weights 400, 700, 900). Creates a traditional print-journalism feel.
- **Sans-Serif Font**: Merriweather Sans. Used for body copy, UI elements, and modern text blocks (variable weight).
- **UI System**: Arial. Used occasionally for small metadata.

## Elevation

- **Flat/Print Layout**: Depth is primarily established through color block separation (switching between `#FFFFFF` and `#FCFAF6`).
- **Subtle Borders**: Items are separated by thin `#E5E5E5` borders rather than drop shadows.

## Components

- **Sticky Navigation Bar**: Clean white or light grey header bar with the distinct red logo.
- **Hero Image Frame**: High-impact editorial photography, often with sharp corners.
- **Highlight Quote**: Important statements often use a larger serif italic font, sometimes inset in a `#FCFAF6` box.
- **News List**: Dense vertical stacks of related news with minimal padding and small thumbnails.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Use `#9F224E` sparingly to highlight critical info (red dots, progress bars, breaking news).
- Emphasize typographic contrast—large serif headlines with sans-serif supporting text.
- Maintain sharp, boxy elements to evoke a real digital newspaper.

### Don'ts
- Do not use heavy drop shadows; keep it flat and clean.
- Do not use gradients or glowing backdrops.
- Do not use playful rounded corners; keep the aesthetic serious and authoritative.
